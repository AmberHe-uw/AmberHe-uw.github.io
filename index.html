Research Teaching Projects Resume

I'm a fourth year undergrad at MIT, studying math and computer science. Upon graduating, I will spent a year as a Resident at Google Brain before starting my PhD in CMU's Machine Learning Department in Fall 2018. Problems that excite me include:

  1. algorithms for learning interpretable models that can reason about their own uncertainty to gaurantee safety
  2. decision making systems that learn to act and to recover from mistakes by interacting with their environment

Over the past year, I've worked on a number of fun research projects with Antonio Torralba and Carl Vondrick in the computer vision group in CSAIL. This past summer, I worked with Jeff Schneider on machine learning for autonomous vehicles at Uber ATC.

On previous breaks from MIT, I've had the pleasure of doing research at Adobe's Creative Technology Lab with Bryan Russell and Josef Sivic, at Xerox Research Centre India with Theja Tulabandhula, at IBM Watson Health with Adam Perer, and at Tsinghua University.
